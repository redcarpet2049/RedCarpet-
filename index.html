About the film

Synopsis

In the hyper-surveilled megacity of 2049, the entertainment industry has merged with government. Marcus Hale — disgraced publicist turned underground journalist — uncovers a hidden signal embedded in every live red carpet broadcast. Decoding it could expose the regime. Ignoring it could cost him everything.

Shot across the neon-lit corridors of a near-future world, RedCarpet 2049 is a relentless sci-fi thriller that asks: when celebrity becomes control, who holds the camera?

Did you know

Trivia

01 — Casting & Naming

The lead role was originally named "Elliot" — Marcus Hale's middle name — before the studio renamed the character in post-production.

02 — Production

Director Soren Mak insisted that every screen visible on set display real-time data feeds, refusing to use static prop graphics. The crew monitored live stock tickers, satellite maps, and broadcast signals throughout filming.

03 — Costume Design

Every costume in the film contains a hidden barcode stitched into the lining — each encoding a line of dialogue cut from the final script, a detail acknowledged by the wardrobe team at the Sundance Q&A.

On location

Filming Locations

⛰

Park City, Utah

Primary Location

The film's grand gala exteriors and climactic broadcast tower sequence were captured across Park City's stark winterscape, doubling as the film's fictional "Apex District" megacity edge.

📍 Confirms Sundance GPS

🏛

Salt Lake City, UT

Studio Interiors

The Ministry of Entertainment headquarters, control room sets, and red carpet arena interiors were all built and filmed inside a repurposed industrial complex in the SLC warehouse district.

🕐

Heber Valley, UT

Act III

The off-grid underground bunker where Marcus decodes the broadcast signal was an actual decommissioned Cold War relay station, used with permission from the property's private owners.
